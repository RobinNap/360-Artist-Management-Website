Invoicing

Create professional invoices and get paid.

To collect your money, you have to make and send invoices to your clients. It is essential to create a complete invoice. You want to make sure the client understands where they are paying for. Make sure you select the right TAX rate for your country/state.

Invoice Essentials

Every professional invoice should include the following key elements to ensure clarity and prompt payment:

  • Your business name and contact information
  • Client details (name, address, contact information)
  • Invoice number and date
  • Clear description of services provided
  • Amount due and payment terms
  • Payment methods accepted
  • Tax information (if applicable)

Best Practices

Following these practices will help you get paid faster and maintain professional relationships:

  • Invoice immediately after completing the service or gig
  • Set clear payment terms (e.g., payment due within 14 days)
  • Follow up politely if payment is late
  • Keep detailed records of all invoices sent and received
  • Use a consistent numbering system for your invoices
  • Double-check all amounts and calculations before sending
