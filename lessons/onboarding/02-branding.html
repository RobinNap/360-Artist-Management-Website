Key Concepts

1 What is a Brand?

A brand is not just a logo or a color palette. It's the complete experience someone has when they encounter you as an artist. Your brand is:

  • The feelings people associate with your name
  • The promise you make to your audience
  • The story you tell about yourself
  • The consistency across all your touchpoints

Think of artists like Daft Punk (mystery, innovation), Billie Eilish (authenticity, edge), or Calvin Harris (party, energy). Their brand is instantly recognizable beyond just their music.

Your brand exists whether you intentionally create it or not. People will form opinions about you. The question is: will you shape those opinions, or leave them to chance?

2 The Brand Pyramid

Think of your brand as a pyramid with multiple layers. From bottom to top:

  1. Core Values: What do you stand for? What principles guide your decisions?
  2. Purpose: Why do you make music? What impact do you want to have?
  3. Personality: If your brand was a person, how would they act, speak, dress?
  4. Visual Identity: Your logo, colors, fonts, imagery style
  5. Touchpoints: How people experience your brand (social media, live shows, merchandise)

The strongest brands start from the bottomâ€”building on authentic values and purposeâ€”rather than starting with just a cool logo.

3 Authenticity vs. Strategy

A common mistake is thinking branding means being "fake" or creating a persona that isn't you. The truth is:

Great branding is authentic AND strategic.

It means:

  • Identifying what's genuinely unique about you and amplifying it
  • Being consistent in how you present yourself
  • Making intentional choices about what to share and what to keep private
  • Understanding your audience and speaking their language

You don't need to pretend to be someone you're not. You need to be the best, most intentional version of who you already are.

4 Brand Consistency

Consistency is what turns a collection of random posts and releases into a recognizable brand. This means:

  • Visual consistency: Same colors, fonts, and style across all platforms
  • Tonal consistency: The same voice and personality in all communications
  • Quality consistency: Reliable standards for everything you release
  • Frequency consistency: Regular, predictable presence

Consistency builds trust. When fans know what to expect from you, they feel safe investing in you emotionally.
